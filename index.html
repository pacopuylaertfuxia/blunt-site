06

Risk
Factors

01

CreaBev® Supplier Dependency

You're reliant on a single encapsulation technology supplier. If they can't scale or pricing increases, the core product differentiator is threatened.

Validate alternative encapsulation options early. Negotiate exclusivity.
02

18-Month Copycat Window

If BLUNT validates the market with a DTC launch, a Nocco, Huel, or Redbull-backed competitor can launch a me-too with 10× the budget. Speed and brand equity are your only moat.

Move fast. Build community before retail. Make brand equity defensible.
03

Retail Timeline Slippage

Independent health stores (Ekoplaza, etc.) have long lead times and listing fees. Phase 3 likely slips to 24–30 months realistically.

Plan cash for a longer powder-first runway. Don't underestimate retail gatekeeping.
07

Recommendation

GO —
Copenhagen
First.
The Angle

Own "creatine for people who don't take supplements." Not gym. Not sport. Not biohacking. Everyday performance for people who care how they feel at 3pm. The cognitive / afternoon energy angle sidesteps the bodybuilder association entirely.

First 30 Days

Lock CreaBev® or equivalent with an exclusivity clause. Design the can before you make the can — the visual identity IS the product validation. Launch a waitlist targeting Copenhagen padel clubs and cycling communities. Get 5 micro-influencers to post about "what they wish existed."

Channel to Study

Momentous — US brand that crossed from elite sport into mainstream with exactly this positioning shift. Their content, ambassador strategy, and DTC-to-retail sequencing is your playbook. Athletic Brewing for cultural proof of concept.

Bottom Line

The concept is sound, the timing is right, the positioning is differentiated. The risk isn't the idea — it's execution speed and staying funded through the 18-month powder → can transition. If the can looks as good as Olipop and the community is tight before retail, this is a real brand.
